## Before the session

**Feature context** *(read this aloud to open the session — do not show them the screen yet)*

> "We're looking at [feature description in 1–2 plain sentences — what it does for the user, not how it was built]. I'd like you to try a few things while I watch. There are no right or wrong answers — I'm here to learn from you, not test you. If anything is confusing, that's useful information for us."

**Remind the participant:**
- Think out loud as you go — say what you notice, what you expect, what surprises you.
- You cannot break anything.
- Ask questions after each task (not during, unless they are completely stuck).

---

## Facilitation rules

- **Do not explain.** If they ask "is this right?", say "what would you expect?" and let them continue.
- **Do not lead.** Avoid "does that make sense?" — it invites agreement. Use "what's going through your mind?" instead.
- **Let them get stuck.** Hesitation is data. Wait at least 10 seconds before intervening.
- **Note the moment, not just the outcome.** Where did they pause? What did they try first? That is often more useful than whether they completed the task.
- **One task at a time.** Do not preview what comes next.

## Wrap-up

After all tasks, ask:

1. "What was the most confusing part of what you just tried?"
2. "What worked well?"
3. "If you could change one thing, what would it be?"
4. "Is there anything you expected to be able to do that you couldn't?"

Thank the participant. Do not ask them to rate the design — qualitative observations are more useful than scores.
