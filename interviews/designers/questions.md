# Designer Interview Questions

These questions are for the first round of designer interviews. The goal is to map a designer's process when handling a PM-written issue with reference screenshots, to identify which steps could be supported by AI tooling.

Share the following context with the designer before starting:

> We're looking at [this issue](https://github.com/String-dxd/teacher-workspace-pg-frontend/issues/35) as a reference case. It was written by a PM with reference screenshots of the existing PG staff portal, but no Figma file or design specs. We want to understand what you would do if this issue landed in your queue, so we can figure out what AI tooling might help an engineer do the same thing without you.

---

## Warm-up

1. When you receive a PM issue like this one (written brief + reference screenshots, no Figma), what is the first thing you do?
2. Do you read the acceptance criteria first, or look at the screenshots first? Why?

---

## Process mapping

3. Walk me through your process from receiving this issue to handing off to an engineer. What are the steps, in order?
4. At each step, what artifact do you produce? (e.g. annotated screenshot, Figma frame, component list, redline doc)
5. Which of those artifacts do engineers rely on most heavily? Which ones do they rarely look at?

---

## Decision-making

6. When you look at a reference screenshot like this, what do you extract that an engineer would not naturally see?
7. How do you decide which component from the design system maps to each element in the screenshot?
8. When the reference screenshot is ambiguous (e.g. spacing is unclear, states aren't shown), how do you resolve it?
9. Are there decisions you make that feel like guesses, or that you'd be happy for an engineer to make themselves?

---

## What's hard without you

10. If this issue went straight from the PM to an engineer with no designer in between, what do you think the engineer would get wrong?
11. What question do engineers most often come back to ask you after they've started building?
12. Is there a type of issue where you think designer input genuinely doesn't matter? What makes those different?

---

## Automation potential

13. Which parts of your process feel the most mechanical or repeatable? (Things you do the same way every time)
14. Are there steps where you're essentially following a rule or checklist, rather than making a creative judgment?
15. If an AI tool could do one part of your job on issues like this one, which part would be most useful to hand off?

---

## Close

16. Is there anything about your process that we haven't covered that you think is important for us to understand?
17. Who else on the design team should we talk to? Is there someone who approaches this differently from you?
